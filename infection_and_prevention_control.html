Infections

Infections, those silent invaders carried by bacteria, viruses, and other unwelcome guests, wreak havoc on our well-being. These tiny organisms sneak into our bodies, multiply, and trigger our immune system's alarm. While some infections cause mild sniffles, others pack a powerful punch, leading to serious illness. But our bodies aren't defenseless fortresses. Our immune system, a complex army of white blood cells and antibodies, stands guard. It recognizes these microscopic foes and launches a counteroffensive, aiming to eliminate them before they cause harm. Sometimes, this battle rages silently, leaving us none the wiser. Other times, the fight manifests as fever, fatigue, or localized pain, alerting us to the ongoing war within.
